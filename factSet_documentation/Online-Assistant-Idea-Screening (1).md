## Where does Company data come from?

Idea Screening provides a seamless view of public and private company data. See [Screening for Companies](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/16956) for more information.

Public company data within companies screening is sourced from FactSet Fundamentals. Learn more in the [FactSet Fundamentals Overview](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/15087) and in [Quick Answers](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/15098).

Private company data within companies screening is sourced from FactSet Private Company. Learn more in the [FactSet Private Company Overview](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/436).

## Are codes available for all the data in Idea Screening?

Some items in Idea Screening have equivalent codes. When a code is available, you will see a link in the item summary. Click the link to launch an Online Assistant page containing the appropriate FactSet Fundamentals code.

However, Idea Screening's search engine itself does is not based on codes, so certain items simply do not have a code equivalent. Additionally, some calculations require using multiple codes at once (e.g., searching on revenue for both public and private companies requires two codes to search FactSet Fundamentals and FactSet Private Company).

## What deals are included in the M&A universe?

The M&A universe draws from FactSet's MergerStat database. This is a global database that includes all deals that fit the following criteria:

-   Both the buyer and seller are disclosed
-   There is a minimum 5% stake
-   The transaction involves the sale of a stake in business operations as opposed to stand alone assets. View the [MergerStat FAQs](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/12530#top) for more information, including to see a list of [excluded transactions](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/12530#exclusion).

Additionally, you can learn tools and tips specific to M&A screening within Idea Screening [here](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/17234).

## Why do I return different transactions for the PE & M&A screens?

The advantage to the Private Equity database is that it provides an ownership-based view of PE investments whereas the M&A database is transaction based. Because of this, you may notice differences in the universe when comparing results between the two content sets. The main drivers of these differences are described below.

In PE/VC but not M&A:

-   A VC investment round is not part of the M&A universe and you did not filter VC out of your search
-   Private Equity that is not a buyout. Can be debt or recaps or other form of minority investment in a new security

In M&A but not in PE/VC

-   Portfolio Company Rollups. If a PE firm uses a child to buy another firm, M&A will tag that as a Private Equity Buyer but PE/VC does not cover that transaction

## How do I know which period these financials are from in Companies screening? It just says latest.

Financials are available for the latest annual period. The corresponding period-end date is available through the Fiscal Year End item.

![](online-assistant/20140.1.html)

All financials for a company are for the same fiscal period. Number of employees may be sourced from a more recent fiscal period, if available. See the [Universe of Companies in Idea Screening](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/16956) for more information.

## How do I download the results?

You can download the results to Microsoft Excel by clicking the **Download** button ![](online-assistant/20140.4.html) in the upper right corner. All downloads include a summary sheet with the screen parameters and a time stamp. Some views have additional download options based on the functionality available in that content set.

Collapsed Views: The option to "Download data without Collapsed Views" will download only the main table. Downloading All Data will download inline views into separate worksheet tabs.

Custom Comps: When working with comps, you can download just the custom comps section (bottom pane), just the screening results (top pane), or both. Each pane downloads into a separate sheet. Deleted companies automatically download into a separate sheet for easy auditability.
