## Using the Truvalue Screen Page 22010

You can access a Truvalue screen within the [Universal Screening](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20593) application in FactSet Workstation and Web.

In the Suggested Screens section, enter "ESG" in the Filter Screens field in the top right section. Click on the "Truvalue SASB ESG Screen" to open the screen.

Results are available in six tables – by default the screen shows Truvalue overall scores. The overall Truvalue screen allows for screening and idea generation, as you can analyze a selected universe for ESG performance, or for improved performance or declining performance.  Those trending performance statuses can be found by comparing ESG Rank to the same value a year before, or by looking at ESG Momentum.

All Categories scores incorporate information across all 26 of these categories, while Materiality scores include only data within categories that [SASB defines as material](https://materiality.sasb.org/) for a company’s industry as categorized by SASB’s Sustainable Industry Classification System™.

Multiple scores in Truvalue data support a full range of investment strategies. The scores include:

|Insight Score|Measures a company's longer-term ESG track record, similar to a ratings system.|
|---|---|
|Pulse Score|Measures near-term performance changes that highlight opportunities and controversies, enabling real-time monitoring of companies.|
|Momentum Score|Measures the trend of company's performance over a trailing twelve-month period.|
|Dynamic Materiality|Reveals the percentage value of data flow for the organization over a trailing twelve-month period.|
|Adjusted Insight|Similar to Insight, the Adjusted Insight score measures a company's long-term ESG track record; however, for low-volume and no-volume companies the Adjusted Insight score uses a blend of the company's score and the industry median.|
|Industry Percentile|Calculated by ranking each company within the SICS Industries using Adjusted Insight.|
|ESG Rank|Indicates if a company is a Leader, Above Average, Average, Below Average, or a Laggard in comparison to other companies in the industry using Industry Percentile.|

The Insight, Pulse, and Momentum Scores are measured on a 0 to 100 scale. A score of 50 represents neutral performance. Scores above 50 indicate positive performance, and scores below reflect negative performance.

For more information on Truvalue Labs ESG methodology and Truvalue formula definitions, see [Truvalue Labs Data Overview](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/21392).
