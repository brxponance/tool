## Searching by Keyword Page 16878

Launch it with FactSet Search: @ISC

Keyword searching in Idea Screening allows you to further narrow your results by entering search terms that appear in descriptions of the entities you are looking for.

For example, Industry Classifications can only narrow a search for formal wear retailers to "clothing retailers." A deeper search requires searching for keywords such as "formal wear," "tuxedo," or "gown."

## Conducting a Keyword Search

Keyword searching allows you to screen against a variety of description sources. Begin by dragging the Business Description (Keyword) parameter into the Search pane.

![](online-assistant/20141.html)

Your keyword search terms take one of three forms:

-   **Simple keyword:** enter a single word to retrieve business descriptions containing that word
-   **Multiple keywords:** enter more than one word on a single line. Idea Screening will retrieve descriptions containing all the words on the line, whether they are adjacent or used individually
    
    |**Tip**|Keyword searches support Boolean logic (e.g., and/or/not) for creating more complex searches. For example, use OR to find at least one of a list of words (e.g., tuxedo OR suit OR uniform) or NOT to exclude a term from the search results (e.g., tuxedo NOT suit). The default behavior is AND.
    |---|---|
    Searching also supports word stemming so that your query returns other relevant descriptions. For example, a search for "tuxedo" will also return results with "tuxedos" and "tuxedoes."|
    
-   **Phrase:** enter a phrase using quotes to find instances where the two words appear together

![](online-assistant/20141.1.html)

Enter your search terms and then click the **Add** button. To edit or delete a search term, hover over the far right edge of the search term's row in the parameter to bring up the **Edit** or **Delete** icons. Clicking **Edit** will remove the row and return the search term to the initial entry form. Once you are finished editing, click **Add** to re-apply the search term.

![](online-assistant/20141.2.html)

After conducting the screen, the results window will specify the number of keywords found in the description, as well as highlight each one. You can then sort the Business Description by the number of matches.

![](online-assistant/20141.3.html)

## Narrowing Searches

### Specifying Descriptions

In many cases, you can search against multiple unique description fields: Business Description, Product Line Description, and, now available for all company types, Web Description. Each of these are indicated in the parameter by its own colored button. Toggling the colored buttons activates or deactivates the sources for a particular search term; deactivated buttons appear greyed out. In the image below, the search will only retrieve listings of "tuxedo" in Business Descriptions, because the buttons for Product Line and Web Descriptions have been greyed out.

![](online-assistant/20141.4.html)

|**Note**|Having all colored buttons active means the search uses OR logic; the term needs only to appear in one of the description styles for you to receive results.|
|---|---|

### Excluding Terms

Similarly, you can entirely exclude a search term from the results by toggling the check box to the left of the term so that it displays as a red X. Doing so will ensure that any description containing the excluded term is not returned. In the example below, the search is excluding any description containing the word “gowns.”

![](online-assistant/20141.5.html)

### Any/All Terms

The Any and All options allow you to specify if you want any of your search terms to match or if all must match for an entity to pass the screen.
