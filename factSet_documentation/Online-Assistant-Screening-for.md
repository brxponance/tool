## Building your Screen

### Screening Criteria

Both public and private companies are included in a company screen by default. You can specify to screen on any combination of Public, Private, or Other companies by selecting or deselecting the appropriate check boxes in the Company Type item. Expand any of the categories to include or exclude subcategories.

You can broaden [your screen](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/16696) by selecting different categories from the Search Items tab.

### Screening on a Specific Company or Portfolio of Companies

Add the Company Name item to your screen and use the type-ahead function to search for a company by name or ticker. You can [add portfolios](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/16697) to your screen by clicking the **Lookup** button and selecting your desired portfolio.

The Company Name item will exclude companies or portfolios if you set the check box to an excluded state.

### Crunchbase Categories and Rank

You can include Crunchbase Categories, which are specific market segments assigned to companies to organize company data and optimize profiles. Companies can belong to multiple categories and category groups. To view a complete list of Crunchbase Categories provided by Crunchbase, see [https://support.crunchbase.com/hc/en-us/articles/360009616373-What-categories-are-included-in-Crunchbase-](https://support.crunchbase.com/hc/en-us/articles/360009616373-What-categories-are-included-in-Crunchbase-).

Crunchbase Rank uses algorithms to score and rank entities (e.g. Company, People, Investors, etc.). The algorithms take into account many different variables, ranging from Total Funding Amount, that entity’s strength of relationships with other entities in the Crunchbase ecosystem, and how many times the entity has been viewed recently.

|**Note**|Crunchbase Categories and Crunchbase Rank only apply to companies shared by Crunchbase and FactSet databases.|
|---|---|

For more information, see [About Crunchbase](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/21470)

## Universe of Companies in Idea Screening

Idea Screening's Private Company screening universe is a more focused collection of FactSet's larger [Private Company](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/436) universe. It contains only companies with relevant, screenable data (e.g., business description and financials) and omits extinct companies. The universe membership consists of over 41,000 public and two million private companies.

When screening against financial data, a private company's financials must be no older than five years from today to return in the result set; public companies' must be no older than three years.

Additionally, when you run a Company screen based on security-level items, Idea Screening returns results based on a company's primary ticker, regardless of where else the security trades. For example, because Bank of China has its primary ticker listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange, it will be left out of a screen searching for companies based in China.

Financials are only available for the latest annual period. The corresponding period-end date is available through the Fiscal Year End item. All financials for a company are for the same fiscal period. Number of employees may be sourced from a more recent fiscal period, if available.

For debt items, the public company coverage is global and the private company coverage excludes the United States and Canada.
