## Filtering Columns

Use filters to specify which universe data you want to appear in your screen results. By default, Universal Screening includes all securities from your universe in report calculations. You can set custom numeric or textual filters to remove outliers or analyze a subset of your overall results.

To apply column filters: 

1.  Choose the Filter tab and select any report column(s) you would like to filter.  
2.  Select an item from the Active Filters pane. Click the adjacent **Remove Filter** button ![](online-assistant/24174.html) to remove it.
3.  If you are applying a filter to a textual column, select its check box in the Column Distribution pane.
4.  If you are applying a filter to a numeric column, hover over the Min: and Max: fields in the bottom of the populated chart, and then click the **Rename** button ![](online-assistant/24174.1.html).
    
    Input your desired range, or drag and drop the slider bars to limit it. All bars with a white background are included in your range; all bars with a transparent foreground are excluded. The dotted black line represents the standard deviation of the selected data item. Hover over each sparkline bar to view specific range values and counts.
    
5.  Once you have applied a filter, it will display a **Filter Selected** icon ![](online-assistant/24174.2.html) in the column header. Your results will automatically update to display only the relevant securities. 

|**Note**|When you apply numeric filters, the counts are updated to reflect how many data items pass the criteria you've selected.
|---|---|
If filters are set on multiple columns, the filtering is cumulative. A security has to pass all of the filters in order to be included in the screen results.|

## Defining Report Groupings

Add groupings to interpret your results from a variety of different perspectives. You can arrange the securities or funds in your report by sector, industry, geography, or existing columns. All grouping controls are flexible and easily accessible. You can reorder the grouping hierarchy, add, edit, and remove groups, and apply other manipulations at any time throughout your analysis.

Expand the Format Results pane and choose the Group tab to get started. Two search boxes are shown by default. From here, there are two methods to group the report: using the type-ahead feature or using the Lookup utility.

### Adding Groupings with Type Ahead

The type-ahead feature makes it easy to sharpen your research and gather the results you need. For example, if you would like to view FactSet Revere RBICS Sector/Industry results, simply enter "RBICS" into the first search box. A list of matching items will be provided within the drop-down menu.

You can also use type-ahead to group by a geographic area or column. Column groupings are searchable when their header, parameter reference name (e.g., PARAM7), and/or formula is specified. Use the different sections (i.e., filters) on the top of the drop-down menu to narrow your view to Sector/Industry, Geography, or Column results. Select the "All" option to view results from all three subsections.

Once you have made a selection in the first search box, you can move onto the second. Click the **Add Another Group** button to apply more than two groupings. Navigate to the Results pane and click the **Refresh** button to apply the selected groupings to your report.

To edit a grouping, click its type-ahead box and search for a different item. The group will be updated automatically when you make a selection.

### Adding Groupings with Lookup

Click the **Lookup** button ![](online-assistant/24174.3.html) to launch a dialog box and browse for groupings, which are organized into specific categories and subcategories (i.e., sources). Choose a category tab in the left pane of the dialog box to see all of the available grouping options.

If you have selected the Sector/Industry or Geography/Other category, select a subcategory to reveal the underlying grouping options. To quickly search for a grouping across all categories and subcategories, type its name into the search box. Further refine your view by including the source name of your desired grouping. For example, "FactSet Sector" will instantly bring you to the Sector/Industry > FactSet > Sector option. If you have selected the Column category, type the name of the corresponding header, parameter reference, formula, or comment into the search box and press **ENTER**.

Select the "All" option to apply all groupings within a subcategory. Each applied grouping will then appear as an individual search box.

Similar to the type-ahead functionality, once you have made a grouping selection, you can move onto subsequent boxes or click the **Add Another Group** button. Click the **Refresh** button in the Results pane to apply the selected groupings to your report.

|**Note**|You cannot select the same grouping option twice. Only eight levels of groupings are allowed. If this number is exceeded, an error will appear in the Results pane.|
|---|---|

|**Tip**|To remove a selected grouping, place your cursor over it and click the adjacent **Remove** button ![](online-assistant/24174.4.html). Click the **Clear All** button in the top right to remove every selection and start over.
|---|---|
Hover over a selected grouping row, place your cursor over the **Drag to reorder** button ![](online-assistant/24174.5.html), and use the drag-and-drop functionality to reorganize the grouping hierarchy.|

### Adding Fractile Groupings

You can group your report into fractiles based on the results of a numerical column, such as Total Revenues, Market Value, and Dividend Yield.

To define fractile groupings:

1.  Follow the steps listed above to browse for and select a numeric data item (e.g., Net Sales) for a potential group.
2.  Place your cursor over the selected column grouping and click the adjacent **Fractile** button ![](online-assistant/24174.6.html).
3.  Select a bucketing option from the drop-down menu to fractile the report by that column.
    -   Custom: Fractiles the component based on the number you specify. Enter a number into the text box or click the up/down arrows to increase/decrease the number. All custom buckets must be greater than 0.
    -   Determined by Data: Fractiles the component based on the column results. For example, all securities that return "1" are placed in one bucket; all securities that return "2" are in placed another.
4.  Click **OK**.

## Sorting Columns and Groupings

Choose the Sorting tab to change how columns, securities/funds, and groupings are displayed. Sortings can be added, removed, and revised at any time, so your screen is as dynamic and relevant as your analysis.

To sort securities by a symbol, name, or parameter:

1.  Choose the Sorting tab, and then click the **Add Another Sort** button in the Sort Securities By section. 
2.  Select the report column you want to sort by from the populated drop-down menu. 
3.  Select "Ascending" or "Descending" from the adjacent drop-down menu. Ascending, which is the default order, presents textual data from A-Z and numerical data from smallest to largest. Descending does the opposite. Non-English textual data is sorted in the order appropriate for that language.

The following example shows a report where securities are first sorted in descending order, by sales growth. They are then sorted in ascending order, by symbol: 

![](online-assistant/24174.7.html)

To sort groupings by a parameter and its statistic: 

1.  Select a parameter from the first drop-down menu in the Sort Groups By section. 
2.  Select a statistic from the second drop-down menu, and then select whether you want the data shown in ascending or descending order. You can only sort by statistics that are used in your report. 

The following example shows a report that is grouped by sectors. The securities within each group are then sorted in descending order, by average market value: 

![](online-assistant/24174.8.html) 

|**Note**|Company Name cannot be removed from the Sort Securities By pane; it is the default sort column. This column appears as the only or lowest level sort unless otherwise specified. Additionally, you cannot select the same sorting option twice. Only 10 levels of sortings are allowed.
|---|---|
Sorting selections will be applied to all group levels. If there is a grouping present, entities are sorted within their group. In addition, only one statistic (e.g., median price) can be used for a group and its sub-groups. Appended securities or funds are treated as a separate table; they cannot be sorted into the screening results.
Groups, securities, and funds that return "N/A" for the sort criteria always appear at the bottom of the sort.|

|**Tip**|If you wish to remove a selected sorting, place your cursor over it and click the adjacent **Remove** button ![](online-assistant/24174.9.html). Click the **Clear All** button in the top right to remove every selection and start over.
|---|---|
Hover over a selected sorting, place your cursor over the **Drag to reorder** button ![](online-assistant/24174.4.html), and use the drag-and-drop functionality to reorganize the hierarchy of the sorting criteria. Each subsequent sorting criterion breaks ties in the prior criteria. For example, if Sector is selected first and Sales is selected second, securities are first sorted by their sector name and then by sales.|

## Adding Column Statistics

Choose the Statistics tab to display summary statistics for the columns in your report.

These items can be applied to any parameter formula that returns a numeric value. The statistic is calculated using formula values for the range of securities or funds in the report that have data available. These statistics are shown at the report level, as well as the [grouping](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20607#Defining) level if you've added groupings to your report.

The Statistics tab has two panes: Select Report Columns and Choose Stats for Selected Column. You can use these panes to modify the statistics shown in the report.

### Selecting Report Columns

Click a column that you would like to apply a statistic to. Press the **CTRL** key and click to select more than one column, or hold down the **SHIFT** key and click to select sequential columns. 

|**Note**|Number Available is the only statistic that can be applied to text columns. All other statistics can be applied to numeric columns.|
|---|---|

To easily find a report column, enter its header or parameter reference into the Filter Columns text box.

### Choosing Stats for Selected Columns

Use the check boxes to apply aggregate statistics for the selected columns. Once a statistic is selected, you can hover over its name to reveal which columns it's referenced in. You can remove all selected statistics for all columns by clicking the **Clear All Stats** button. To remove statistics for a particular column, click the down arrow in its heading, select Statistics, and deselect the check boxes of all undesired statistics. Click **Done**. 

The following check boxes are shown when a statistic is applied to:

-   ![](online-assistant/24174.5.html) - All of the selected columns. Single-click this to remove the statistic from all of the selected columns.
-   ![](online-assistant/24174.10.html) - One or some of the selected columns, but not all. Single-click the check box to apply the statistic to the rest of the selected columns.
-   ![](online-assistant/24174.11.html) - None of the selected columns. Single-click to add the statistic to all of the selected columns.

Click the **Refresh** button in the Results pane to apply the selected or removed column statistics to your report. The name of the chosen column statistic will appear within the first column and row of the Results pane. Statistical values for each of the selected report columns will appear within their respective columns.

## Applying Formatting Options

You can use formatting options to customize how data is displayed in your report. 

### Showing/Hiding Columns

Determine which columns are shown or hidden. Hover over a report column in the Filter, Statistics, and Format tabs and click the ![](online-assistant/24174.12.html) icon to hide it. To show a hidden column in your results, click the icon again. The last show or hide action you perform will be applied. 

|**Note**|All hidden columns will still be calculated.|
|---|---|

### Specifying Decimal Places in the Format Tab

Type the number of decimal places you want displayed for the formulas in each selected column. The default decimal place for float and double data is 1. 

### Adding Column Dividers 

Add a divider after any column by selecting the "Insert Divider After" check box. A divider line will appear in the [Results pane](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20608) and in Excel and PDF [downloads](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/21010#downloading) of your screen.
