## Understanding Universe Categories Page 20687

The following describes each of the category tabs available in equity screens.

Enter all or part of a universe limit into the Add Criteria text box at the top of the [Select Criteria](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20606) pane. The type-ahead functionality will quickly return a list of matching results. Use your cursor or keyboard arrows to locate and select your desired security, or click on a filter to limit your search results to a particular category. You can also click the **Browse for Criteria** button ![](online-assistant/25850.html) and select universe limits from the Qualitative or Quantitative tab. 

### Index/ETF 

Limit your universe to all securities in a specific index (e.g., S&P 500) or index family (e.g., all MSCI Developed indices). Exchange Indices, such as the U.K. FTSE 100 or the German DAX 30, are also available in this category, as well as exchange-traded fund constituents (e.g., SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust). 

![](online-assistant/25850.1.html)

|**Note**|ETF constituent limits are only accessible through type ahead; they are not available via the Browse button. On the other hand, top-level index families (e.g., all of MSCI indices) are only available via Browse. They are not accessible through type ahead.|
|---|---|

### Sector/Industry 

Limit your universe by FactSet-defined or database-specific sectors and industries. 

### Geography 

Limit your universe to a region, country, or state. 

### Formulas, Functions, Custom Formulas

Limit your universe to all companies that pass a formula-defined limit. Click Browse or use the type ahead to find predefined formulas (e.g., Sales) or functions (e.g., Average), or the custom formulas you have saved in your [Personal](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/3935#personal_library),  [Client](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/3922#client_library), or [Super\_Client](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/3938#super_client_library) directory. 

You can use these formulas to create custom limits with logical operators, such as >, <, and =.  

### Exchange 

Limit your universe to all companies on a given trading exchange, such as the New York Stock Exchange, London Main Market, or Tokyo Exchange. The exchange names are sourced by the Pricing vendor and are based on the formula [P\_EXCHANGE](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/16610).

### Security

Limit your universe by security type. See [FactSet Symbology Security Type Descriptions](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/15776) for a full breakdown of each security option available.

### Identifier 

Limit your universe by specific identifiers. You can add or remove them from your report.  

### Database

Limit your universe to companies listed in a specific [OFDB](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/3934#ofdb) file or online identifier list. 

|**Note**|Database limits are currently limited to the **Browse for Criteria** button ![](online-assistant/25850.html). They are not accessible through type ahead.
|---|---|
If you have a subscription to [Debt Screening](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20888), you can also base your equity screen results off of a previously saved debt screen. Select a debt screen from your [Personal](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/3935#personal_library), [Client](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/3922#client_library), or [Super\_Client](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/3938#super_client_library) directory to add it as a criteria item.|

### Broker 

Limit your universe to securities covered by a specific broker(s) or analyst(s). This category is only available if you subscribe to [FactSet Estimates](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/13369) detail-level estimates.

|**Note**|The Broker category is not compatible with the "Set Backtest Date" feature, which is available via the **Screen and Application Settings** menu ![](online-assistant/25850.2.html). In addition, the category can only return current coverage. It should not be used with screens intended to be used with quantitative applications (e.g., Alpha Testing).|
