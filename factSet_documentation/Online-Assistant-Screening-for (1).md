## Screening for Transactions, Buyers, or Advisors

M&A screens return a list of transactions, either for analysis as individual transactions (e.g., find comparables, find precedents) or in aggregate (e.g., top advisors, top industries, average multiples).

You can perform a search for buyers, which will screen for potential buyers of companies or private equity investors. You can also screen for potential advisors of firms and search for IPOs and follow-on bookrunners given a set of criteria.  

Select your screening type from the "View" drop-down menu. After running the report, you can then pivot your search results from a list of transactions to a list of buyers (and vice-versa), as well as [Volume Totals and League Tables](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/17281) by changing this drop-down selection.

## Applying Criteria to Specific Participants

You can choose to apply Target and Acquirer (e.g., industry, location, etc.) to a specific participant using the options available on each item. For example, find all acquisitions involving a U.S. target (e.g., a U.S. private company is sold) or find all transactions involving any U.S. company on the target or sell-side (e.g., a UK company sold its U.S. subsidiary).

![](online-assistant/20142.4.html)

### Target/Sell-side Participants

Apply the criteria specifically to the target or to any sell-side participant in the transaction. The default is Target.

-   **Target:** Apply the criteria to the target in the transaction (i.e., the company, asset, or division being acquired). For example, in 2001, General Mills, Inc. acquired The Pillsbury Company from Diageo PLC. In this transaction, The Pillsbury Company is the target. This transaction would be returned if the selected criteria applied specifically to The Pillsbury Company.
-   **Any Sell-side Participant:** Apply the criteria to any participant on the target-side (i.e., sell-side participant). Sell-side participants include the target and, where applicable, a target's parent, a private equity firm exiting the target company, the private equity firm's related fund, and additional sellers (e.g., significant minority stakeholders). In the above example, both The Pillsbury Company and Diageo PLC are sell-side participants (target and target parent respectively). This transaction would be returned if the selected criteria applied to either The Pillsbury Company or Diageo PLC.

### Acquirer/Buy-side Participants

Select to apply the criteria specifically to the acquirer or to any buy-side participant in the transaction. Default is Any Buy-side Participant.

-   **Acquirer:** Apply the criteria to the acquirer(s) or bidder(s) in the transaction – the actual company or entity making the acquisition. For example, in 2004, Clayton Homes Inc., a subsidiary of Berkshire Hathaway Inc., acquired Oakwood Homes Inc. In this transaction, Clayton Homes Inc. is the acquiring company. This transaction would be returned if the selected criteria applied specifically to Clayton Homes Inc.
-   **Any Buy-side Participant:** Apply the criteria to the any participant on the acquirer-side (i.e., buy-side participant). Buy-side participants include the acquirer and, where applicable, an acquirer's parent and additional bidders (e.g., private equity firms, financial buyers). In the above example, both Clayton Homes Inc. and Berkshire Hathaway Inc. are buy-side participants (acquirer and acquirer parent, respectively). This transaction would be returned if the selected criteria applied either to Clayton Homes Inc. or Berkshire Hathaway Inc.

## Finding Specific Transactions

You can screen for a particular transaction using the Deal ID/Lookup criteria. Type any combination of Target name, Acquirer name, Announcement Date and/or Deal Identifier to isolate specific transactions.

Begin typing to see matching transactions. The more terms you include, the finer your search results. Click a transaction to select it:

![](online-assistant/20142.5.html)

|**Tips**|Click the **Lookup** button ![](online-assistant/20142.6.html) to import a portfolio of previously saved deal IDs.
|---|---|
You can also use the [Find](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/16699#find) feature to search within your screening results for a specific transaction.

The additional reports can include the [Deals - Snapshot](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20759) report, the company report for the target or acquirer, and underlying source documents.
