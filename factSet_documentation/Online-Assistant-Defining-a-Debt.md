Debt Screening lets you access the fixed income databases offered on FactSet. Screen on specific coupon rates and credit ratings on over two million active securities. Limit your universe of bonds by type of security, geographical location, and more. Plus, you can use a variety of fixed income analytics to dig deeper and make conclusions about issue performance.

## Getting Started

To get started, click the **Debt** button located at the top of the application [Home](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20595#home) page.

You can choose from the following options in the Suggested Screens section:

-   Click the **New Debt Screen** button ![](online-assistant/26070.html) to create a blank report. All corporate, government, and agency issues are included in the default universe.
-   Click the **Starter Screen** button to get some basic result columns on the default debt universe.
-   Click a predefined debt screen (e.g., Recent Moody's Downgrades) to open a report that has a FactSet-established universe and format. Each demonstrates the main features of the application and can serve as building blocks for creating your screens.

## Managing Universe Limitations

Use the Select Criteria pane to specify your debt universe. If you are creating a new screen, the Select Criteria pane will be expanded by default. If you selected a predefined screen, the universe is already selected, but you can adjust that as needed.

![](online-assistant/26070.1.html) 

Select your universe by applying any of the fields in the top category tabs shown. The category options and definitions for debt screens are as follows:

-   <u>Issuer</u>: Use this tab to specify the characteristics of bond issuers (e.g., country of risk).
-   <u>Bond Details</u>: Choose this tab to select criteria pertaining to issues and their terms.
-   <u>Risk</u>: This tab covers credit risk specifications including seniority as well as S&P and Moody's credit ratings.
-   <u>Analytics</u>: Select this tab to measure the performance of issues using a variety of metrics. 
-   <u>Redemption</u>: Use this tab to define the embedded options and repayment terms included in your universe (e.g., all convertible bonds).
-   Identifier List: This tab allows you to limit your screen by a set of securities (e.g., indices, watch lists, and portfolios) that are stored in Identifier Lookup or your saved OFDB file/s.
    
    |**Note**|Only fixed income indices covered by the FG\_CONSTITUENTS formula are available via Identifier Lookup. 
    |---|---|
    If you have a subscription to [Equity Screening](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20606), you can also base your debt screen results off of a previously saved equity screen. This is especially helpful for performing credit analysis on an equity portfolio or equity coverage list. Click the **Lookup** button ![](online-assistant/26070.2.html) in the right pane of the Identifier List tab to get started.|
    

### Browsing for Limits

Hover over each category pane and scroll down its list menu to see specific criteria options such as Issuer Type and Seniority. There are also form fields and fields with drop-down menus. Use the up and down arrows or type in a number to customize each metric such as Yield and Amount Outstanding.   

![](online-assistant/26070.3.html)  

### Adding and Removing Limits

Enter or select an item to include it in your universe and add it to the top of the Select Criteria pane. When available, select a category's "All" option to include every item listed in it. 

Modify your criteria by removing individual issues. To remove an item from your current limitations, click the adjacent **Remove** button ![](online-assistant/26070.4.html). Additionally, you can click the **Clear All** button in the top right corner to remove all of the debt screening criteria.

![](online-assistant/26070.5.html)  

### Excluding Inactive Bonds

The "Exclude Inactive Bonds" option removes all bonds that have matured or are otherwise inactive as of today (or backtest date if set), and also bonds whose issue date is more than one month into the future (or backtest date). By default, this option is selected when you create a new screen or open a Debt Starter Screen.

![](online-assistant/26070.6.html)

|**Note**|The "Exclude Inactive Bonds" option does not exclude perpetual bonds that have been called or redeemed.|
