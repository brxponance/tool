## Adding and Adjusting Calculations 

Universal Screening lets you search for, add, and modify your parameters in seconds. You can also use [special variables](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/21266) instead of entering dates, [OFDB](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/3934#ofdb) file names, formulas, or part(s) of a formula in your parameters. 

### Browsing Data Items

The **Browse for Formulas** button ![](online-assistant/25676.1.html) allows you to search and select the formulas you would like to bring into your report. You can restrict your search by name or concept (e.g., Closing Price or Estimates) or by typing or selecting the appropriate category in the left pane. 

Once you locate the data item you want, you can change the entire formula, its arguments (e.g., currency), and view a preview of the syntax. Additional methodology and source details are also shown. 

### Adding New Parameters 

The **Add New** button allows you to add [new screening parameters](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20605#Add_New) to your report. Find a formula by typing a keyword or phrase into the search box and clicking a matching item. By default, you will see a list of formula matches from all of the databases that you have access to in order of relevance. 

### Locating Parameters

There are a number of ways in which you can locate a parameter you have added. The type-ahead functionality matches any text you enter against the parameter's [reference name](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20608#Reference) (e.g., P1), [column heading](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20608#Reference), formula description, or index (i.e., row) number. 

For example, typing "ROW5" into the Add Columns workspace will return the parameter that is listed in the first index of your [Parameter view](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20608#Parameter), followed by any parameter that has the number 5 as part of its heading, formula name, or reference name. 

![](online-assistant/25676.2.html)

### Modifying Parameters 

Click an [existing data column](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20605#Edit_Existing) you want to modify and click within the Add Columns workspace to access the Arguments tab. Select different arguments or apply a function, then click the **Modify** button to update the formula (i.e., and its column results) with the applied changes.  

|**Tip**|You can also use these screening tools to conduct comparative analysis. If you are modifying an existing parameter, you can always click **Add New** to apply your changes as a new column. Once clicked, the new column will appear beside the one you were initially modifying.|

## Updating Report Column Layouts 

Each column houses multiple options that allow you to seamlessly update how your screen results are displayed. There are six default columns in the starter screen: Company Name, Company Symbol, Closing Price, Market Value, Net Sales, and RBICS economy. 

|**Note**|These features are part of the default Results view and may not be part of the Charting and Parameters views.|
|---|---|

### Editing Header and Reference Names 

Select "Edit Heading" from the column drop-down menu to customize your result view. This tool allows you to add another layer of flexibility for your columns so they are easy to manage and remember.

![](online-assistant/25676.3.html)

You can use these to create a more descriptive, intuitive header than the default. 

|**Note**|Header names have a 500-character limit. Reference names must range from 2-31 characters and contain at least one letter. Numbers and underscores are also valid. Both names are reflected in the [Parameter](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20608#Parameter) and [Result](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20608#Result) views.|
|---|---|

### Applying Filters and Groupings 

Refine your results by [filtering](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20607#Displaying) or [grouping](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20607#Defining) based on the options available for the selected column. If you have already applied a filter in the Format Results pane, the **Filter** icon ![](online-assistant/25676.4.html) will appear with a check mark. This indicates that a filter has been applied to that column. If you have an applied grouping, all of its constituents (and their results) will appear beneath it. Use the **Expand/Collapse** buttons ![](online-assistant/25676.5.html) to view or hide the underlying values of each grouping within the Parameter view. 

To apply a new filter or grouping, click the column and make your changes in the Format Results pane. Alternatively, you can click the column's drop-down arrow and select "Group" or "Filter" to group by the column or apply a filter. Once you are finished, navigate to the Results pane and click **Refresh** to update your screen. 

### Sorting and Ordering Columns 

Quickly [sort](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20607#Sorting) column values in ascending or descending order by double-clicking its header. This can also be achieved by selecting "Sort" from the header drop-down menu. Drag your selected column right/left to change the column order. Company Name and Company Symbol columns cannot be moved; they are always the first two listed. 

Easily resize your default column widths by hovering over the column divider, clicking it, and dragging to the desired location.

### Applying Statistics

Select "Statistics" from the drop-down menu to [apply any statistic](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/20607#Adding) to a parameter that returns a numeric value. Additionally, you can apply the Number Available statistic to any parameters with formulas that return text. The statistic is calculated using values returned for companies in the report that have data available. Statistics are displayed at the report level and also at the group level if you've added a grouping to your report. 

|**Tip**|You can also right-click on a column header to: 
|---|---|
-   [Deactivate or recalculate it](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/21898#Deactivating) 
-   Copy or cut a column
-   Insert a copied or cut column 
-   Remove a specific column entirely 
-   Edit the heading content shown|

## Toggling Result, Charting, and Parameter Views 

Universal Screening offers three different ways to view your report. 

### Result View 

The Result view is the default view. It provides the results of the parameters and limitations that you have selected. 

Click on a security name to view identifiers and exchange information, or access links to next-step reports such as [Guide](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/16976), Snapshot, and [Economic Synopsis](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/17688). You can even click on the sector or industry hyperlinks to launch an [Idea Screening](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/16590) report to quickly view sector or industry comparables. 

![](online-assistant/25676.6.html)

Hover over each available sparkline bar to view specific range values and counts. To modify these inputs, select "Filter" from the column's drop-down menu. 

**Analyzing Results in Other Applications**

Double-click a result to share it with other applications (e.g., [Interactive Charting](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/17580), [News](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/15863), and the [Company/Security tab](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/17450)) within the FactSet Workstation and perform a multi-dimensional analysis.  Screening's Identifier Broadcast functionality allows you to view and analyze data for securities passing your screen without leaving the application or launching Identifier Lookup.  

![](online-assistant/25676.7.html) 

To blast an identifier using Identifier Broadcast, right-click the tab of your choice and select "Identifier Broadcast" > "Receive Only." Once you double-click an identifier in Screening, it will automatically be blasted to that tab's ID widget. 

![](online-assistant/25676.8.html)

### Charting View 

Launch the Charting view to create and export on-the-fly charts based on your parameters. To get started, click on the **Charting** button ![](online-assistant/25676.9.html) and start making your selections within the populated dashboard. See [Charting Your Results](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/21125) for more details. 

![](online-assistant/25676.10.html)  

### Parameter View

The Parameter view lists the number of securities or funds passing your screen, along with the report parameters. To get started, click on the **Parameters** button. See [Viewing Parameters](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/21898) for more details.
