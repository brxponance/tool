## Which GICS Grouping Definition Should I Use?

GICS classifications provided by [MSCI](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/220), [Standard & Poors (S&P)](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/13542), and S&P/Citigroup come directly from the index databases. Similarly, the Australia - GICS groupings cover the companies in the [Standard & Poors Australian (S&P/ASX) Indices](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/14276). These groupings are updated daily and offer complete historical access. For an index, these grouping definitions ensure that every security has a classification (no securities are in the Unassigned category). However, securities that are not in the index family are not classified.

Compustat provides GICS groupings for all securities in its database. It offers broad coverage for U.S. portfolios and benchmarks, but the data is current only, so inactive companies are not categorized.

GICS Direct is a partnership between Standard & Poors and MSCI. They provide official current GICS classifications for a global universe. An additional subscription is required to access historical data, which is available for U.S. securities, Canadian securities, and [ADRs](https://my.apps.factset.com/oa/pages/pages/3920#ADR) only. Historical access for other non-U.S. securities is not provided.

If you select "GICS Multi-Sourced" as your grouping definition, FactSet classifies securities based on the various GICS databases that you have access to. Coverage of securities varies based on your subscription, but this grouping is current only.
